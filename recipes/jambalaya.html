Taylor's Jambalaya

Description

Inspired by my visit to New Orleans, I set out to make my own version of this great creole classic. This jambalaya recipe is very traditional, tastes great, and is simple to make. Make a pot for your family or bring it to a party with friends and you will surely impress!

Ingredients

  • 1 package (12-14 oz) andouille sausage
  • 2 1/2 pounds chicken thighs
  • 2 large onions
  • 1 cup celery (diced)
  • 1 large green pepper
  • 1 bunch green onions
  • 2-3 tablespoons of your favorite cajun seasoning
  • 1 10 oz can of rotel tomatoes
  • 3 1/2 cups chicken broth
  • 2 cups white rice
  • 3 tablespoons olive oil
  • Dutch oven or a large pot

Steps

  1. Cut the sauasge into 1/4" rounds and cube the chicken thighs.
  2. Dice the onions, green pepper, celery, and green onions.
  3. Put the pot on the stove over medium high heat and add half of the oil. Brown the sausge and chicken thighs. The chicken should not be fully cooked this step is just to brown the chicken.
  4. Remove the chicken and sausge from the pot while keeping as much oil in the pot as possible. Add the diced onions and stir continuously until a deep golden brown. (12-15 minutes)
  5. Add the green pepper and celery and continue stirring for 2-3 minutes or until softened. Add the entire can of rotel tomatoes and continue stirring. Make sure nothing sticks to the bottom of the pot.
  6. After 1-2 minutes, add all of the chicken broth, white rice, and cajun seasoning.
  7. Continue stirring until the broth begins to simmer. Reduce the heat to low and put the lid on the pot.
  8. Let the jambalaya simmer for 20-25 minutes. Enjoy!
